VocalWarmups

Warming up your voice before you sing is not optional — it protects your voice and improves your performance. Here's what we recommend.

Why Warm Up?

Your vocal cords are muscles. Singing on a cold voice risks strain and injury. A proper warmup loosens the muscles, improves tone, extends your range and prepares you mentally to sing well. 10–15 minutes before rehearsal or a service makes a real difference.

Basic Warmup Routine
  1. Breathing — 2 mins. Deep diaphragmatic breaths. Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4.
  2. Lip trills / bubbles — 2 mins. Run through your range on a lip trill. Relaxes the face and engages support.
  3. Humming — 2 mins. Gentle hum up and down scales. Keeps the voice easy and forward.
  4. Sirens — 1 min. Smooth glide from lowest to highest note and back. Opens the full range.
  5. Arpeggios on vowels — 3 mins. "Mah-may-mee-moh-moo" on a 5-note arpeggio, moving up by semitones.
  6. Tongue twisters — 1 min. "Red lorry, yellow lorry" or "unique New York" for articulation.

Looking After Your Voice
  • Drink plenty of water — hydration is everything for vocalists
  • Avoid dairy and cold drinks before singing
  • Rest your voice if you're unwell — singing through illness causes damage
  • Avoid shouting or talking loudly the night before a big singing commitment
  • Steam inhalation can help if you're congested
  • If you experience consistent pain when singing, see a vocal coach or ENT specialist
Vocal Coaching

If you'd like to improve your voice and technique, we'd encourage you to invest in regular vocal coaching. Speak to the Worship Site Co-ordinator for recommendations of vocal coaches who work with worship singers.
