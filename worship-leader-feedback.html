For worship leaders

FeedbackJournal

A reflection journal to help you grow as a worship leader. Use it after leading a service.

What is this?

This is a tool to help you develop as a worship leader.

How can I help this to be a success?

You have to commit to the process, block out some time where you can think about your worship leading, answer the questions honestly and fully, and be open and ready to receive constructive feedback.

How frequently should I complete this journal?

You can use this tool on a regular basis after leading worship, particularly in a larger setting like Sunday mornings. It will form the basis of your development alongside some coaching.

How long should I take to complete the journal?

It is best to make notes after each time you lead. The journal is not meant to take a lot of time to complete - you can use bullet points or notes. You don't need to fill all the space for your thoughts and reflections - even just one thought in each section is helpful.

Do I have to answer all the sections?

This process will work best if you answer all the sections. If you do not understand what is being asked or struggle to put your answers into words then don't worry - we can discuss it when we meet.

Can I include worship leading in other settings?

If you feel it will help in your development then yes you can. For example, it may be that you feel more comfortable trying something out in a smaller group setting at first. On the whole, though, most of the examples should be from larger settings.

What happens after I complete the form?

We will discuss this as part of the coaching process. The focus of the discussion will be to help you develop your skills.

What happens to this form?

This form is yours to keep as a development journal. Other notes may be used as part of the coaching process.

2. Rehearsals

Reflect and comment on your leading of rehearsals. We are looking to develop as musicians, worship leaders, and our skills leading a band. Think about the following in terms of developing the band:

  • Building authentic relationships within the band
  • Appropriate and creative use of different instruments
  • Effective shaping of songs
  • Contrast within songs
  • Development of vocal harmonies
  • Use of dynamic variations
  • Use of rhythmic variations
  • How we start and finish songs
  • Structure and effectiveness of rehearsals
  • Communication and team working between worship leaders
