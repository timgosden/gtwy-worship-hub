WorshipLeaders

Resources, guidelines and tools specifically for worship leaders at Gateway Vineyard.

Planning Your Set

Here's how leading worship at Gateway works:

  1. One week before - plan the journey of the set:
    • Gathering / Call to worship — pulling everyone in
    • Revelation — who God is and what he's done for us
    • Response / Encounter / Intimacy — inviting the Holy Spirit, surrendering to God
  2. Pick keys that are a good pitch for your voice, allow the set to flow and are singable for the congregation. The Worship Site Co-ordinator can help with this.
  3. Submit your set list (including CCLI numbers) and band lineup and requirements to the Production Team at your site no later than five days before the service:
    • Central — via WhatsApp group
    • Chapel — via email
  4. If you're unsure or need help, just ask.
Song Selection
  • Mix of newer songs and well-known congregational favourites
  • Consider the arc of the set — building, dwelling, responding
  • Check the Song Bank for approved songs
  • If you want to introduce a new song, speak to the Worship Site Co-ordinator first
  • Avoid clustering too many new songs in one service
Sunday Morning
  • See the Call Times page for arrival times
  • Sound check before doors open — be ready to go
  • Connect with the service leader before the service starts
  • Keep transitions smooth — silence kills momentum
  • Hold things loosely — the Spirit may take you somewhere different
Pastoral Care of the Team

As a worship leader you're not just musical — you're pastoral. Check in on your team, create a safe and encouraging atmosphere and bring anything that needs attention to the Worship Site Co-ordinator.

Growth and Reflection

One of the ways we grow is by reflecting on previous services. Each week there's an opportunity to watch the live stream back and reflect on how things went. These questions might help:

  1. How did the congregation respond?
  2. What significant things was God doing?
  3. How was your musicality and/or singing?
  4. Did you lead the band effectively, or were there moments of uncertainty?
  5. What encouragement can you bring to the team following the service?
  6. What challenges or obstacles did you face during the soundcheck or service, and how did you overcome them?
  7. How did you engage with the congregation, and were there moments where you could have improved your communication or connection with them?
  8. What did you learn from this experience, and how can you apply it to future services?
