Songwriting

We believe God is still writing new songs through His people. Here's how we support and develop original songwriting at Gateway.

Why We Write

Every song in the modern worship canon started with someone writing what God was putting on their heart. We want to create a culture where songwriting is encouraged, developed, and celebrated — not just as a creative pursuit, but as a spiritual discipline.

Getting Started
  • You don't have to be a seasoned songwriter to start — just start
  • Write about what God is doing in your life right now
  • Study the songs your congregation loves — why do they connect?
  • Read the Psalms for lyrical inspiration and structure
  • Record voice memos of ideas as they come — don't trust memory alone

Sharing Your Songs
  • If you've written something you'd like the team to hear, share it with Tim
  • Be open to feedback — songwriting in a church context is collaborative
  • Original songs can be introduced to the congregation once they've been developed and approved
  • We want to own our own sound as a church — your songs matter
Songwriting Sessions

We aim to run occasional songwriting sessions for the team. Watch out for announcements. If you'd like to arrange an informal writing session with others on the team, let Tim know and he'll help connect people.
