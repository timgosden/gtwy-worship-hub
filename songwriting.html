Songwriting

We're so encouraged to see God moving in our homegrown songs. Here's how we steward that gift together.

Our Goal

Our goal is not that every song written across the life of Gateway is sung on a Sunday morning, but that we explore all that God has for us in songwriting - both creatively and prophetically - creating intentional spaces to reflect and grow in our gifts.

Step 01
Discernment

Not every song written at Gateway will be suitable for corporate worship. Broadly, songs tend to fit one of the following categories:

  • Personal devotion
  • Corporate worship and exaltation
  • Item or theme specific (e.g. Christmas or Easter)

Every song has value, even if it's never sung congregationally. Our goal as writers is to write out of the overflow God has given us and bring our offering to the body.

Step 02
Feedback

Songs will be shared with a group of songwriters and leaders from across our sites for encouragement and feedback. We aim to meet monthly to review submissions, looking at both practical and spiritual aspects: structure, syllable count, note range, themes, theology and so on.

Feedback will be provided in a timely manner and will aim both to encourage the writer and to critique the song. Like any gift, we believe songwriting must be practised and refined.

Step 03
Workshopping

Songwriting can be deeply personal, especially when it flows from our own relationship with God. But like any craft, there's always room to grow. Where possible we'll provide specific, helpful feedback on areas the group feel could be worked on.

We encourage co-writing where possible - having another songwriter come alongside can help to refine and improve a song beyond what you could achieve on your own. We get the privilege of growing together.

Step 04
Next Steps

Once a song has been workshopped, you're welcome to resubmit. It's at this point that an indication of whether something is a "Gateway song" will start to become clear. The likely outcomes are:

  • Further encouragement and feedback - keep writing
  • An invitation to redraft with additional feedback
  • The opportunity to create a more finished demo of the song
  • The opportunity to bring the song into a wider Gateway setting - small groups, worship events or Sunday services
