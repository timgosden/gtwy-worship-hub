The team

TeamRoles

Everyone on the worship team has a part to play. Here's what each role involves and what's expected.

What All Team Members Share

Whatever your role, these responsibilities apply to everyone on the team:

  • Personal practice — developing your musical skill regularly between services
  • Personal worship — spending time in God's presence, not just practising songs
  • Checking the rota and contacting the Worship Site Co-ordinator as soon as possible if changes need to be made
  • Attending team meetings and rehearsals
  • Arriving on time, set up and ready to play by the agreed call time
  • Helping with PA set-up relevant to your instrument or microphone
  • Bringing new songs to the attention of the Worship Site Co-ordinator
  • Taking every opportunity to set an example in worship to others
What We're Looking For
  • Committed Christian, part of a Life Group and an established member of Gateway Vineyard Church
  • Committed to the Vineyard Worship Values of Intimacy, Accessibility, Integrity, Kingdom Expectation and Passion
  • Willing to work as part of a team
  • Proficient as a singer or musician

Training is tailored to the individual in discussion with the Worship Site Co-ordinator. External training is available where appropriate.

Specific Roles
Worship Leader
Leads the congregation in sung worship. Responsible for song selection (in consultation with the senior team), leading rehearsal and being spiritually prepared to guide the congregation in encounter with God. Usually plays guitar or keys. Supervised by the Worship Site Co-ordinator.
Lead Vocalist
Sings lead on songs when not leading worship, or supports the worship leader in carrying the melodic front of the band. Strong pitch, blend and the ability to lead confidently from the microphone.
Backing Vocalist
Provides harmonic support and atmospheric backing. Key role in filling out the sound and supporting the congregation in singing. Requires good blend, strong pitch and the ability to take direction quickly.
Acoustic Guitar
Provides rhythmic and harmonic foundation. Usually DI'd direct. Strong rhythm playing is essential — worship guitar is about locking in with the band and serving the song, not soloing.
Electric Guitar
Adds texture, atmosphere and melodic leads. Requires a good ear for what a song needs and the restraint to leave space. Pedalboard and effects knowledge a plus.
Keys / Piano
Fills harmonic space and adds pads, piano parts and synth textures. Often the glue of the band. Keyboard players need to be comfortable playing in a full-band context and in stripped-back scenarios.
Drums
Drives the rhythm and energy of the band. Must be able to play dynamically — from quiet, intimate moments to full-band anthems. Click track experience required. We use an electronic kit.
Bass Guitar
Locks in with the drummer to form the rhythmic backbone. Clean, supportive playing that underpins the band. DI'd direct to the desk.
