TeamRoles

Everyone on the worship team has a specific role. Here's what each one involves.

Worship Leader
Leads the congregation in sung worship. Responsible for song selection (in consultation with the senior team), leading rehearsal, and being spiritually prepared to guide the room in encounter with God. Usually plays guitar or keys.
Lead Vocalist
Sings lead on songs when not leading worship, or supports the worship leader in carrying the melodic front of the band. Strong pitch, blend, and the ability to lead confidently from the microphone.
Backing Vocalist
Provides harmonic support and atmospheric backing. Key role in filling out the sound and supporting the congregation in singing. Requires good blend, strong pitch, and the ability to take direction quickly.
Acoustic Guitar
Provides rhythmic and harmonic foundation. Usually DI'd direct. Strong rhythm playing essential — worship guitar is about locking in with the band and serving the song, not soloing.
Electric Guitar
Adds texture, atmosphere, and melodic leads. Requires a good ear for what a song needs and the restraint to leave space. Pedalboard / effects knowledge a plus.
Keys / Piano
Fills harmonic space and adds pads, piano parts, and synth textures. Often the glue of the band. Keyboard players need to be able to play both in a full-band context and stripped-back scenarios.
Drums
Drives the rhythm and energy of the band. Must be able to play dynamically — from quiet, intimate moments to full-band anthems. Click track experience required. Electronic kit used on the platform.
Bass Guitar
Locks in with the drummer to form the rhythmic backbone. Clean, supportive playing that underpins the band. DI'd direct to the desk.
Media / ProPresenter Operator
Runs lyrics and visuals via ProPresenter during the service. Must be present at rehearsal and the full service. Requires attention to detail, the ability to follow the worship leader in real time, and familiarity with ProPresenter 7.
